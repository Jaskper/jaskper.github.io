CHICAGO (Reuters) - On Friday, BMZ Capital Partners shocked Wall Street by acquiring ZLC Investments. According to Hedge Fund Research, ZLC Investments is a little-known quantitative hedge fund based out of Chicago. Although ZLC boasts a team of superstar academics, its returns have been remarkably poor. The fledgling fund has seen capital outflows since its inception in 2018.

BMZ Capital Partners conducted a sophisticated leveraged buyout of ZLC, which valued the failing firm at 0.01 USD - a remarkable 40% premium to its market value. However, BMZ Capital Partners has confidence in the acquisition. BMZ's CFO Sammy Zeng remarked, "We acknowledge that it is obviously a risky play given ZLC's poor track record, but we are hopeful that ZLC's team can live up to its potential." Similarly, BMZ's Chief Researcher Michael Bian expressed optimism: "Look, we've known thse guys for years. We used to tear them apart every week in poker during University. But we think they might have learned a thing or two from watching us in poker and the markets, so we do believe that we can turn them around."

ZLC's website can be found here: zlcinvestments.com.
